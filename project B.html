Origin

West Coast rap originated in the late 1970s and early 1980s, primarily in Los Angeles, California. While hip-hop started on the East Coast, West Coast artists developed their own distinct sound, influenced by funk, gang culture, and social issues. Origins & Key Figures: Ice-T (1980s) – One of the first West Coast rappers to gain recognition, pioneering gangsta rap with songs like 6 in the Mornin’. N.W.A (Late 1980s) – With Straight Outta Compton (1988), they brought gangsta rap to the mainstream, addressing police brutality and life in South Central L.A. Dr. Dre & G-Funk (1990s) – Dr. Dre’s The Chronic (1992) popularized G-Funk, a smoother, funk-inspired rap style. Tupac Shakur (1990s) – Became one of the most influential rappers with deeply personal and political lyrics. Snoop Dogg, Ice Cube, E-40 – Expanded West Coast rap’s influence with unique flows and storytelling. West Coast rap is known for its laid-back yet aggressive style, funky beats, and themes of street life, social struggle, and partying.
