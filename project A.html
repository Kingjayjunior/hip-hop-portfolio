Origin

East Coast rap originated in the 1970s in New York City, particularly in the Bronx, where DJs and MCs started experimenting with breakbeats and rhyming over instrumentals. Some key moments and figures in its early development include: DJ Kool Herc (1973) – Considered the "father of hip-hop," he pioneered breakbeat DJing at block parties. Grandmaster Flash & The Furious Five (Late 1970s) – Innovated turntablism and brought storytelling into rap. Run-D.M.C. (1980s) – Helped bring hip-hop to the mainstream with their fusion of rap and rock. The Notorious B.I.G., Nas, Wu-Tang Clan (1990s) – Defined the golden era of East Coast rap with lyricism, storytelling, and gritty beats. East Coast hip-hop is known for its emphasis on lyricism, complex wordplay, and boom-bap production style. It has evolved over the decades, influencing modern rap worldwide.
